




1. Executive Summary
Modern Identity and Access Management (IAM) systems rely on static, rule-based policies that cannot adapt to evolving threats. They fail in two critical ways: they block legitimate users too often (false positives), and they allow attackers with stolen credentials to pass through undetected (false negatives).

This project builds an AI-powered identity risk engine that learns each user's unique behavioral patterns — their login rhythm, device loyalty, location consistency, and resource access habits — and encodes it into a Behavioral DNA Fingerprint. Every login attempt is scored against this fingerprint in real time. If the behavior does not match the identity, access is challenged or blocked, even when the password is correct.

4. Unique Feature — Behavioral DNA Fingerprinting

4.1 What Behavioral DNA Is
A Behavioral DNA Fingerprint is a fixed-length vector that encodes a user's typical patterns across six behavioral dimensions simultaneously. It is computed from the user's verified historical sessions and updated on a rolling basis.


4.2 DNA Match Scoring
Every new login generates a live behavioral snapshot and computes similarity against the stored DNA across all six dimensions. The result is a DNA Match Score (0–1). A low match score directly increases the risk score, even when the password is correct.

4.3 DNA Drift Detection — The Truly Novel Part
Behavioral DNA is not static. It evolves as users' lives change. The system distinguishes between natural evolution and suspicious takeover by measuring drift velocity — how fast the DNA is changing — and how many dimensions change simultaneously.


Key insight: Natural life changes affect one behavioral dimension at a time. An account takeover changes everything simultaneously because the attacker is a completely different person.
4.4 DNA Confidence Score
DNA is not equally reliable for all users. The system tracks confidence based on data volume and consistency. New users get lenient DNA matching. Established users with 90+ days of history get strict matching. The system tells you how much to trust its own DNA assessments.
